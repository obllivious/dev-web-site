In the different definitions found in the published literature on these terms, with particular reference to the proposed definition of UNESCO, we can highlight some relevant characteristics in each one:

   
  • Science - It is a set of organized knowledge; It develops through time; Part of mechanisms of causality of observable facts; It is based on the objective study of empirical phenomena; It is related to the proof of theories; It is associated with the publication of articles, theses, books, etc.; The created knowledge is freely transmitted and patrimony of civilization.

  • Society - It is constituted by a group of people; It develops through time; It is based on a system of relationships between people; Its members share purpose, interests, concerns and customs and interact with each other, constituting a community; Its members build knowledge and develop technology.

  • Technology - It is a set of scientific or empirical knowledge; It develops through time; It is based on the application of knowledge in the production or improvement of goods and services; It is related to socioeconomic impacts on a community; Its results are obtained from the application of new materials, processes, methods and products in the means of production; It is in tune with economic development and the well-being of society; Its products and processes can be negotiated and framed as patents.

    ORGANIZATION FOR ECONOMIC CO-OPERATION AND DEVELOPMENT (OECD)

    The Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) is an international and intergovernmental organization that brings together the most industrialized countries in the market economy, based in Paris, France. Through the OECD, member country representatives come together to exchange information and define policies to maximize economic growth and development of these countries in the current Information and Knowledge Society.
